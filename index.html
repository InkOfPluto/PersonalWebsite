Research

Haptic Slip Rendering for Improved Grasp Stability in VR Teleoperation (MSc Thesis)

  • Developed a full VR–haptics closed-loop system integrating Unity/C#, custom fingertip hardware, and serial communication. Conducted a controlled user study (N=21) with over 2M frames of data. Results showed ~30% faster first success with haptics, angular error reduced from ~45° to ~18°, and multimodal feedback improved user confidence (NASA-TLX >6 vs. ~4.5 unimodal).

Remote Robot Grip Guidance using Haptic Rendering

  • Co-authored and implemented the RemoteHapticGrasp system that mapped fingertip slip cues into VR teleoperation. Designed ESP32-based haptic thimbles and Unity simulation of grasp stability across 15 random CoM conditions. User study revealed visual feedback alone often misled participants, while haptic cues improved grasp direction, and combined visual+haptic achieved best stability.

User Study & Analytics Toolkit

  • Created a reusable analytics pipeline for interaction experiments. Includes regression and statistical models for recovery time, angular error, and completion rates. Delivered visualizations (radar, error bars, regressions) to evaluate perception, workload, and success metrics across multimodal conditions.
